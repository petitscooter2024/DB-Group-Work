Part A: Project Overview

1. Project Idea Definition and Detailed Explanation

Project Topic

The planned project entails the design of a database system of an Auto Parts E-Commerce Platform in Kenya. The system is to handle the information on automotive spare parts, supplier information, customer orders, and inventory information to facilitate the online sales and the effective distribution of parts.

Problem Being Addressed

Automobile spares business in Kenya has a few challenges:

* Problems with finding the right spare part with a certain vehicle model.
* Restricted online systems of buying auto parts.
* Ineffective inventory control in auto part stores.
* Absence of central databases between suppliers, garages and customers.

the majority of the dealers in spare parts continue to use manual records or simple spreadsheet which causes mistakes in stock management, wrong part orders and delays in the delivery of services.

In addition, vehicle owners would not easily locate the right spare parts on time, which leads to long repair schedules and higher expenses.

Context in the Kenyan Environment

Kenya is an auto market with broad prospects of growth, especially in urban areas like the Nairobi City, which have thousands of imported second hand vehicles that need periodical maintenance and replacement of spare parts.

Although digital commerce is an upsurge in Kenya, the auto parts industry is mostly fragmented and offline. The majority of the transactions are conducted in the physical shops or informal networks and it is not easy to compare price, availability and even compatibility by the customers.

A large auto parts database that is linked to an e-commerce can enhance transparency, accessibility and efficiency in the chain of supply of spare parts.


Relation to Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

This project supports SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure.

The system contributes to this goal by:

* Promoting digital transformation in the automotive supply chain.
* Improving efficiency in parts distribution.
* Supporting small auto parts businesses with better inventory management.
* Encouraging technological innovation in Kenya's automotive sector.

How the Proposed Database System Helps Solve the Problem

The proposed database system will act as the core infrastructure of the e-commerce platform by:

* Storing structured information about vehicle models and compatible parts
* Managing inventory levels across suppliers
* Enabling efficient search and retrieval of spare parts
* Supporting customer order processing
* Maintaining accurate transaction records

By organizing automotive parts data into a structured relational database, the system will reduce errors, improve searchability, and enable a scalable online marketplace for spare parts.


2. Scope and Objectives

Scope of the System

The system will focus on managing automotive spare parts data and transactions within an online marketplace.

The system will include:

* Storage of vehicle and spare parts information
* Supplier and inventory management
* Customer account records
* Order and purchase transaction management
* Search functionality for finding compatible parts

The system will NOT include:

* Physical logistics and delivery management
* Vehicle repair service scheduling
* Payment gateway processing (handled by external payment services)



 Objectives of the Database System

The main objectives of the proposed system are:

1. To create a centralized database for automotive spare parts information that allows efficient storage and retrieval of parts data.

2. To improve inventory management for auto parts suppliers by tracking stock levels and availability.

3. To support an e-commerce platform for buying and selling auto parts by managing customer orders and transactions.

4. To improve accuracy in identifying compatible auto parts for different vehicle models.



Main Operations of the Database System

The database system will support several key operations including:

* Storing spare parts records
* Retrieving parts based on vehicle compatibility
* Managing supplier and inventory information
* Recording customer orders and purchases
* Updating stock quantities after transactions
* Managing user account information
